Innovation is young people working for free

Jan 24, 2016

Do-it Connect is a 'volunteering search engine' that received) £48,500 of the Innovation in Giving Fund a few years ago. Here's Do-it's self-description:

However, Do-it.org has seen a change in the expectations of both volunteers and organisations. *Volunteers are increasingly interested in shorter, one-off opportunities and organisations want to recruit volunteers with specific qualities* [emphasis added]. The demand for brokering these relationships is growing so for the first time, Do-it.org will introduce a new search function. It will enable organisations to search, find and connect to volunteers.

The meaning there is fairly clear, these 'volunteers' weren't looking for projects to get stuck into for long term work in the community, they're young people looking for unpaid work in the hope of finding paid work after their 'one-off' opportunity ends. Likewise organisations weren't looking for just anyone to pitch in - they wanted people with "specific qualities". This means a job description for unpaid positions.

Looking through the site a lot of the volunteering seems legit

I think we should be concerned by groups treating that "change in the expectations" as something that is just a natural thing that happened. In reality the sector was reorientating itself to better exploit the youth labour market.
